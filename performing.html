Performing Arts

My Performing Arts Experience

Theatre
In elementary school I had to perform two class plays every year. They were usually musicals that told silly stories, like a retelling of the American Revolution, or Disney movies, like Lilo and Stitch and Peter Pan. I did not particularly enjoy them.
Dance
I took dance classes for seven years. The genres I learned included traditional Chinese dance, ballet, and jazz.
Singing
Throughout elementary school I had to sing on a regular basis for music class, so I was somewhat skilled at singing. Then I began middle school, where we no longer had mandatory music classes, so I stopped singing. Without practice, I got progressively worse at singing. In addition, my voice started changing and getting lower, so my vocal range is no longer suited to singing anyway.
Musical Instruments
I have experience in playing the piano, violin, and recorder. I played the piano for about eight years in the past, but I quit before high school due to the amount of time it took. I started playing the violin six years ago, and I still play it now. I played the recorder during fourth and fifth grade because it was part of the music curriculum.

My Preferences in Performing Arts

I enjoy listening to classical music, especially symphonies and concertos from the Romantic era. I prefer the style of Romantic era pieces because they are more expressive compared to Baroque and Classical era compositions.

I used to also be interested in pop music from the last decade, particularly songs about deep or dark topics made with VOCALOID engines. I initially began listening to VOCALOID music because most of it was in a language I didn't understand; it was good for background music while studying. I continued to be interested in it because I enjoyed the instrumentals as well as story-telling style of certain producers.

Now I also listen to K-pop artists whose songs are recommended to me, like BTS, Blackpink, Mamamoo, and Dreamcatcher.
